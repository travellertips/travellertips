If you want to visit this great medieval castle, your start point should be Stuttgart. From there you should take the regional train to Hechingen. If you want a direct train, this could be IRE 3253 like in my case and you should plan it good, as it departures approximately every 2 hours. The ticket price was 19 EUR for me. You can check and book the tickets here.

Hechingen is very quiet small town. If you go to the back of the station you can already see the castle on the hill. There are two ways to get the castle - shuttle bus (every hour) or a taxi (~15 EUR). In my case it was a taxi, as I had not much time and planned to visit another German castle. Taxi or shuttle bus drives you to the parking platform that is about 10 minutes from the castle. There you can use the toilet and find some snacks as well as the tourist information, tickets to the second shuttle to the castle. You have two options to go: by foot or by shuttle bus. I would recommend a bus, as you’re going to save some power to go to the view point ;)

In the castle you’ll manage to buy tickets, you can eat at the restaurant in the castle, have a guided tour. It is very interesting in the castle itself. Also, if you're hungry, there is a nice small cafe in its territory - you can drink some coffee and look into the distant hills.

But afterwards you would want to go here:

" alt="Hohenzollern castle view" />

So, let’s go to a magnificent view!

When you go out the gates of the castle and start to go back by the shuttle’s road, in 200-300m you would see the small arrow sign pointing left off the road in the forest. You should follow the signs with Mariazell (first checkpoint of our route) or Zellerhorn (our destination point) wordings. If the weather is rainy or you go right after the rain - be prepared, the forest path is very slippery.

So, our first point is

Hechingen-Boll, Mariazell
Unnamed Road, 72379 Hechingen

This is what you expect to see there:

So our destination point is
