Get to the exclusion zone without the permit

by Grygorii Polinovskyi

29 August 2017
Pocket travelguide

If you are about stalking — you can easily get to the Exclusion Zone in the Chernobyl region. This is not a Chernobyl itself, but the place is within the 30km alienation area and already a place to feel the Stuff. And… may be you meet some mutants there, who knows ;)

Please note that this trip can be quite tricky. Official law says: "During passing the Zone you should not leave your vehicle, otherwise you will violate the law". But you always have an option... you know, you wanna pee ;) Or you can drive through without stops which will any way be an experience.

Our destination is Polisske city - an abandoned town in the Chernobyl Exclusion Zone, part of Kyiv Oblast in Ukraine:

Open in Google maps

Of course, if you go there, you should be kind of prepared. First of all, you should be aware — that this is an exclusion zone, thus there is a remaining radioactive fallout contamination. However, if your goal is to be shiny at night — this is the place to be! Ok, joking — the risk is not high in this region. It is also known that there are some people living in the ghost city (round ten people). Because it’s a closed area there are a lot of wild animals and flocks of dogs. I would recommend to have something like sound-device to scare dogs (you can buy at aliexpress) or some fireworks.

The trick to get the place without an official permission is that the 7km of the regional road (P02) goes through the Zone itself.

If you go there, you should go by some transport and better to be accompanied with ukrainian (or russian-) speaking person because you will have a short talk at the checkpoint: you probably will be asked for your documents and your destination. You just say that you go to the Ovruch city (if you start from Kiev) or vice versa. You can also say that you’re a special american agent if you want to have some fun :) Ok, better not, as our goal is an abandoned town. At this point you should hide all your cameras, as making photos are officially prohibited. However, when I was there I got a word permission to get some snaps from that guys at the checkpoint. So, things are not that scary and serious as you can first think.

As soon as you pass the checkpoint you will get to the quiet ghost town... If you want to get more experience — you should get off the way and leave your car in a way that it’s hidden for other road users.

I really recommend this place, as this is that kind of places that stay with you forever!

Traveller tips

When is the best time to visit

It depends on your preferences — if you go there to feel some darkness and make the photos that are emotionally strong — then a late autumn or winter is the best time.

Equipment to have

Special equipment is not required, but remember that the region is wild for many years. It is better to have some dog repeller or some fireworks that make sound.

Helpful vocabulary

  • Hello — Добрий день (dobryj den’)
  • I am driving to Kiev — Я їду до Києва (ya yidu do Kyjeva)
  • How much does it costs? — Скільки це коштує (Skil’ky tse koshtuje)
