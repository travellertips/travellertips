History of the Castle

One of the most beautiful medieval castles of Germany is Hohenzollern Castle. The history of the 750 years-old castle is quite interesting. First mention about the place dates to the 11th century. Before it was destroyed completely in 1423, the Castle had a very prominent value for the region. It was called "Crown of all Castles in Swabia". The second Hohenzollern Castle that was more fortified and bigger started its life in 1454. During the Dark Ages it changed many owners and got ruined till the beginning of 19th century.

From 1850 King Frederick William IV started reconstruction of the Castle. One of his ideas was to make this place habitant again, which is still the case till our days. Currently the castle is a private property of Hohenzollern dynasty.

The view of the castle is spectacular! Its gothic style reveals some unknown romantic feeling. The landscape around makes the place even more attractive to visit.

How to get the place

If you want to visit this great place, your start point can be Stuttgart. From there you should take the regional train to Hechingen. You should plan your day in advance, as the direct regional train departures approximately every 2 hours.

Price for the train ticket from Stuttgart to Hechingen was 19 EUR for me. You can check and book the tickets here.

Hechingen is very quiet small town and there is no problem to find the right direction. When you step out from the train, you can go to the back of the station — from there you already see the castle on the hill. There are two ways to get the castle: shuttle bus (departures every hour from the bus station that is right near the railway station) or a taxi (~15 EUR). In my case it was a taxi, as I had not much time and planned to visit another German castle next morning. Another benefit to take a taxi — is that you can ask driver to stop on the spot which is great for a nice photo. Taxi or shuttle bus drives you to the parking space that is about 10 minutes from the castle. There you can use the toilet and find some snacks as well as the tourist information. And yes, I am writting here about the toilet. Ha-ha. When you travel, sometimes it's a hell to find a "rest place". There are two options to get the castle from parking: by foot or by castle shuttle bus. Ticket for the shuttle to the castle costs 2 EUR for an adult. I would recommend a bus, as you’re going to save some power to go to the view point ;)

In the castle you can go for a guided tour or walk yourself. If you're hungry or desperately in the need of a cup of coffee, you can sit at small caffee on its territory and eat some snack there. You should follow the signs with "Mariazell" (first checkpoint of our route) or "Zellerhorn" (our destination point). If the weather is rainy or you go right after the rain — be prepared, the forest path is very slippery.

Traveller tips

When is the best time to visit

If you want to have great photos of the place, I would recommend to go in autumn (because of colors) or in winter with snow.

How you get the place

From Stuttgart, take regional train IRE (check the connection at bahn.de). From Hechingen railway station take a shuttle bus (approx. every 2 hours) or taxi. You can use special tickets if you travel in group or want to visit Stuttgart region, like metropolticket.de or Baden-Württemberg-Ticket.

If you drive by car…

Other useful information and time tables you find on the website of Hohenzollern Castle

Kind of clothes you should pack

If you travel in a warm time it’s better to have one or two additional T-shirts. As you climb to a photo-spot it could be quite warm in the forest.

The way you can go around

It is quite simple to get a place with public transport, but if you like to travel with car, here are awesome resource to find one in the region: Rent a car in Stuttgart.

Suggested daily budget

Assuming you travel with public transport only, have food in fast-food restaurants, your minimal day budget will be around 56 EUR.

This includes:

  • Public transport, including regional train connections: (Stuttgart-Hechingen-Stuttgart): 24 EUR if you use Baden-Württemberg-Ticket (21 EUR if you are under 27 years old).
  • Food, if you eat in middle-class or fast-food restaurants - 20 EUR
  • Castle entrance ticket (7 EUR - entrance to the castle's area, 12 EUR - visit also interior rooms of the castle, so we assume MAX-program here ;) )
