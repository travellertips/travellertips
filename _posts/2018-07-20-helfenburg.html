In the northwest of Czech Republic, forgotten in the forest the ruins of the 14th Century castle have their rest. The beginnings of the castle are dated before 1375, when Hanuš of Helfenburk sold this castle to the Archbishop of Prague named Jenštejn, as known from the written sources. To be honest, this guy sold not only the castle, but also the surroundings with forests, villages and towns in the administration of the castle.

The Czech name of the castle — Hrádek, whose remains give only a weak view on the real castle standing here more then 600 years ago. As one of many castles from that time, its first appearance was mostly as a wooden fortification, however made with all known castle's attributes like a moat with water and high walls around the residence.

The reconstructions and adjustments made after 1375 changed the appearance of the castle entirely. Even the roads and the entrances were changed. The main entrance was made secure — it was quite complicated to get into the castle without getting into trouble.

During the times of political instabilities and epidemies, the fortification for young archbishop Jenštejn was finished. After he survived the illness and finished his political career, the Helfenburk which was previousely reserved for his retirement, became his refuge.

During the 15th Century, the castle has changed its owners, was sold and bought many times, served as a residence or as a storehouse of treasures and sacred objects. In November 1620 it was burned down during the Thirty Years War that was a first step to its desctruction and oblivion. Only in 1887 it became a new life by its owner Josef von Schroll. Starting from 1970s it is maintained by civic association "Hrádek" and open vor visitors till today.

It will be easier to get the place if you have your own (or rented) car. Your destination point will be a parking lot aside of the road, marked with the Helfenburk sign near the small settlement Ostré (near Úštěk), Czech Republic.

From the other side, the public transport connections are also ok. You can get to the place by train from Prague. The trip will last around 2 hours and cost 7 EUR one-way ticket. You can check how to get the place below in our Tips section.

From there you will start your trip through the forest to reach the castle. As soon as you have parked your car, you can go to the opposite side of the road and search the white-red or later on the way, white-yellow signs on the trees. Like these:

The way through the forest is not difficult and you can get the place within 20-30 minutes without a big hurry. Path splits several times and first goes down a hill and after crossing small river goes up again. The place is full of mystery. The Legend says that people got lost in the forest in the past times catched by the ghosts…

But no worries just follow the signs and you will be save. The place is quite friendly with the number of visitors.

At the end of the way castle appears first as ruins on the hill which gives peculiar feelings of the past times.

The place is not very secured for the visitors, there are a lot of places where you might fall down to the ditch or hurt yourself by other means. So be careful, especially if you are traveling with kids.

In the castle you will probably meet some other travelers walking around, having rest or even making some barbecue on the open fire. You can even go the very top of the tower to get a great view.
